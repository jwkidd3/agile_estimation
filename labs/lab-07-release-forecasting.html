🔮 Lab 7: Release Forecasting Workshop

Module 7 — Agile Project Estimation | Duration: 80 minutes (60 min work + 20 min presentations) | Style: Capstone Group Workshop

🎯 Lab Objectives

  • Build a release forecast collaboratively using multiple techniques
  • Practice both estimate-based and fact-based forecasting as a team
  • Reach group consensus on buffer strategies for different risk profiles
  • Prepare and deliver a stakeholder-ready forecast presentation as a group

👥 Group Setup — Capstone Team Exercise

Form groups of 4-5 people. This is the capstone lab — your group will work together through every step, combining all the skills practiced throughout the course. Forecasting is a team responsibility that requires collective input, shared assumptions, and unified communication. Assign roles:

  • Product Owner: Advocates for scope and business priorities in buffer decisions
  • Scrum Master: Facilitates the forecasting process, ensures data-driven decisions
  • Analysts (remaining members): Run calculations, challenge assumptions, prepare the stakeholder presentation

Tools & Presentation

Choose your tools: Your team decides how to work — whiteboard, spreadsheet, digital tool, paper, or any combination. Use whatever works best for your group.

Final deliverable: Each group presents their complete forecast to the class as a simulated stakeholder meeting (5 minutes per team). The class acts as the executive audience — they will ask tough questions.

Technique 2: Thin-Slice Recommendation

Instead of committing to a date now, write a recommendation to the CEO for a "thin-slice" approach. What would you propose for the first 2 sprints, and when would you provide a confident forecast?

3 Buffer Decision Exercise (Group Consensus)

10 minutes

As a group, debate and decide on a buffer strategy. The Product Owner should advocate for scope protection while the Scrum Master raises process risks. The team must reach consensus — buffer decisions affect everyone.

Buffer Type Size Your Recommendation Justification
No buffer 0 sprints
Small buffer (10%) ~1 sprint
Medium buffer (20%) ~2 sprints
Feature buffer Cut 15% of scope

Risk Factors to Consider

  • The team lost a developer in Sprint 6 (replaced in Sprint 7)
  • A major integration with a 3rd-party payment processor is still unestimated
  • Holiday season will reduce velocity for 2 sprints
  • Scope growth rate has been averaging ~5 points/sprint

Which buffer strategy do you recommend and why? Consider both time and feature buffers.

6 Team Presentations — Stakeholder Simulation

20 minutes (5 min per team)

Each team presents their complete forecast to the class. The class acts as the executive leadership team. Cover:

  • Your forecast (with and without history) — show your work
  • Your recommended buffer strategy and justification
  • Your quality metrics analysis
  • Your stakeholder-ready forecast summary

After each presentation, the "executive audience" asks questions: "Can you move the date up?" "What if we add more people?" "What's the risk if we skip the buffer?" The presenting team must respond as a unified group.
