🔮 Lab 7: Release Forecasting Workshop

Module 7 — Agile Project Estimation | Duration: 50 minutes (35 min work + 15 min presentations) | Style: Capstone Group Workshop

🎯 Lab Objectives

  • Build a release forecast using multiple techniques
  • Understand how forecast ranges produce more reliable date commitments than single-point estimates
  • Deliver a stakeholder-ready forecast that communicates uncertainty honestly

👥 Group Setup — Capstone Exercise

Form groups of 4-5 people. Assign: Product Owner (scope priorities), Scrum Master (facilitates), Analysts (calculations and presentation).

Deliverable: Each group presents their forecast as a simulated stakeholder meeting (3-5 min per team).

3 Date-Driven Forecast & Stakeholder Presentation

15 minutes

The Date Is Set

Your CEO has returned from a board meeting: The board has committed to a live product launch at a major industry conference in 20 weeks (10 sprints). This date is tied to a contractual obligation with your largest client and cannot move.

Using your calculations from Step 2, work backward from the fixed date.

Backward Calculation

CalculationYour Answer
Available sprints before the deadline10
Max deliverable points (available sprints × avg velocity from Step 2)
Expected scope growth over 10 sprints (at your calculated avg rate)
Net deliverable points (max deliverable − expected scope growth)
Remaining backlog points (from Step 2)
The gap: remaining backlog − net deliverable points

Scope Decision: What Makes the Cut?

If the gap is positive, you must cut scope to hit the date. Categorize the remaining backlog:

CategoryPointsFeatures Included
Must-have for launch (demo-critical)
Should-have (include if velocity allows)
Defer to post-launch release

Risk Factors

  • The team lost a developer in Sprint 6 (replaced in Sprint 7)
  • A major 3rd-party integration is still unestimated
  • Scope growth has averaged ~5 points/sprint — will the PO freeze scope for the final push?

Stakeholder-Ready Output

Conference launch date: [fixed — 20 weeks out]

Scope we can deliver with high confidence (85%): ___________ points
Scope we can deliver if things go well (50%): ___________ points
Must-have features confirmed for launch: ___________
Features deferred to post-launch: ___________
Key assumptions: ___________
What would cause us to re-forecast: ___________

If the CEO asks "Can we deliver it all by the conference?": Present your data — max deliverable points vs. remaining backlog. Show the gap. Offer three options with their scope implications: (1) freeze scope growth, (2) reduce launch scope to must-haves only, (3) add capacity. For each option, state what the launch will and will not include.

4 Team Presentations — Stakeholder Simulation

15 minutes (3-5 min per team)

Each team presents their forecast to the class (acting as executive leadership):

  • Your backward calculation — max deliverable points vs. remaining backlog
  • Your scope decision — what makes the launch and what gets deferred
  • Your stakeholder-ready output — confidence levels and assumptions

"Executives" ask tough questions: "Can we do it all?" "What if we add people?" "What happens if scope keeps growing?" Respond with data — show the gap and present options with their scope implications.
