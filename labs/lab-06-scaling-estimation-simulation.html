🏢 Lab 6: Scaling Estimation Simulation

Module 6 — Agile Project Estimation | Duration: 65 minutes (50 min work + 15 min presentations) | Style: Group Workshop

🎯 Lab Objectives

  • Simulate multi-team estimation and coordination challenges collaboratively
  • Experience cross-team dependency management firsthand as interconnected groups
  • Practice aggregating metrics across teams through inter-group communication
  • Debate backlog organization strategies as a full class — consensus across teams

👥 Group Setup — Multi-Team Simulation

This lab is a full-class simulation. The class splits into 3 teams to experience the coordination challenges of scaling Agile. Each team works independently on estimation but must coordinate with other teams on dependencies — just like in a real scaled Agile environment. Cross-team communication is essential.

Tools & Presentation

Choose your tools: Each team decides how to collaborate — whiteboard, sticky notes, spreadsheet, digital tool, or any combination. Cross-team coordination can use whatever medium works (in-person stand-ups, shared board, etc.).

Class presentation: At the end of the lab, each team presents their estimates, dependency findings, and forecast. Then the full class discusses the backlog strategy debate together (3-5 minutes per team).

1 Team Formation & Setup

5 minutes

Split the class into 3 teams. Each team represents a Scrum team working on the same product: a Healthcare Patient Portal.

👥 Team Alpha — Frontend Experience

Responsible for: Patient-facing UI, mobile responsiveness, accessibility

Average velocity: 26 pts/sprint

👥 Team Beta — Backend Services

Responsible for: APIs, data processing, integrations with hospital systems

Average velocity: 30 pts/sprint

👥 Team Gamma — Platform & Infrastructure

Responsible for: Security, compliance, deployment, monitoring

Average velocity: 22 pts/sprint

5 Team Presentations & Cross-Team Debrief

15 minutes (3-5 min per team + class discussion)

Each team presents to the class. Cover:

  • Your team's estimates and where dependencies forced sequencing changes
  • Which team was the bottleneck and how you would address it
  • Your recommended backlog organization strategy

After all teams present, compare: Did the 3 teams' sprint plans actually align? Where did cross-team assumptions conflict?

💡 Discussion Points

  • How did cross-team dependencies change your sprint plans compared to single-team planning?
  • Can you compare story points across teams? Should Team Beta's "8" equal Team Alpha's "8"?
  • What coordination ceremonies would you add for this multi-team setup?
  • How would feature teams (cross-functional, full-stack) reduce dependency problems?
