📦 Lab 3: Story Decomposition & INVEST Workshop

Module 3 — Agile Project Estimation | Duration: 75 minutes (60 min work + 15 min presentations) | Style: Group Workshop

🎯 Lab Objectives

  • Collaborate as a team to decompose epics into themes, features, and user stories
  • Apply the INVEST criteria collectively — leveraging diverse perspectives to improve stories
  • Build a group product backlog with proper story hierarchy through consensus
  • Identify spikes as a team — uncertainty is best surfaced through group discussion

👥 Group Setup

Form groups of 4-5 people. Your group is the Scrum team for this exercise. Story decomposition and backlog grooming are inherently collaborative activities — a single person writing stories misses the cross-functional perspective that makes Agile estimation effective. Assign roles:

  • Product Owner: Represents business value, prioritizes the backlog
  • Scrum Master: Facilitates discussion, keeps the group focused
  • Developers (remaining members): Provide technical perspective on feasibility and decomposition

Tools & Presentation

Choose your tools: Your team decides how to collaborate — whiteboard, sticky notes, index cards, spreadsheet, digital tool, or any combination. Use whatever works best for your group.

Class presentation: At the end of the lab, each team presents their decomposition, INVEST findings, and prioritized backlog to the class (3-5 minutes per team).

3 Spike Identification (Team Discussion)

10 minutes

As a group, review the backlog you've created and identify areas where a spike or "Get Smart" story is needed. Uncertainty often hides in assumptions — challenge each other to surface what the group doesn't know.

Indicators That a Spike Is Needed:

  • The team cannot estimate a story (too much uncertainty)
  • A technology choice has not been made
  • Integration with an external system is unfamiliar
  • Performance or scalability concerns need investigation

5 Team Presentation to Class

15 minutes (3-5 min per team)

Each team presents to the class. Cover:

  • Your theme structure and decomposition approach
  • Your best and worst INVEST examples — and how you fixed the failing stories
  • Your prioritized backlog and any spikes you identified

Compare backlogs across teams — how did different groups decompose the same epic?

💡 Discussion Points

  • What was the hardest part of decomposing the epic?
  • Which INVEST criterion do you find most commonly violated in real projects?
  • How do spikes reduce estimation risk?
  • How would you handle the "hidden waterfall" if stories were assigned by function (all design first, then all development)?
