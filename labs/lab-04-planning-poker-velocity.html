🃏 Lab 4: Planning Poker & Velocity Calculation Workshop

Module 4 — Agile Project Estimation | Duration: 75 minutes (60 min work + 15 min presentations) | Style: Group Workshop

🎯 Lab Objectives

  • Practice a full Planning Poker estimation session as a Scrum team
  • Experience relative sizing firsthand — estimation is a team sport
  • Collaboratively calculate team velocity from historical sprint data
  • Work together to build and communicate release forecasts

👥 Group Setup

Form groups of 4-6 people — this is your Scrum team for the entire lab. Planning Poker only works as a group activity; the value comes from diverse perspectives revealing hidden complexity. Assign roles:

  • Product Owner: Reads stories aloud, answers clarifying questions about business intent
  • Scrum Master: Facilitates the poker session, ensures fair process, tracks votes
  • Developers (remaining members): Estimate, discuss, and challenge assumptions

Tools & Presentation

Choose your tools: Your team decides how to run Planning Poker — physical cards, fingers, a digital tool, sticky notes, or anything else. For velocity calculations, use whatever works: whiteboard, spreadsheet, pen and paper. The method is your choice.

Class presentation: At the end of the lab, each team presents their estimates, velocity calculations, and forecast to the class (3-5 minutes per team).

2 Planning Poker Session

25 minutes

Estimate the following backlog items using Planning Poker. Follow the process:

  1. Product Owner reads the story aloud
  2. Team asks clarifying questions
  3. Everyone simultaneously reveals their estimate
  4. High and low estimators explain their reasoning
  5. Re-vote if needed, then record the consensus

4 Group Reflection

10 minutes

Discuss these questions as a team — the retrospective is where estimation improvement actually happens.

What did your group observe during the Planning Poker session? Where did the biggest disagreements occur, and what did the discussion reveal about hidden assumptions?

As a group, discuss: Why is "yesterday's weather" (using recent velocity) often more reliable than a 6-sprint average? How would your team communicate the forecast range to a stakeholder?

5 Team Presentation to Class

15 minutes (3-5 min per team)

Each team presents to the class. Cover:

  • Your story point estimates — where did your team have the biggest debates?
  • Your velocity calculations and release forecast range
  • How you would communicate the forecast to a stakeholder

Compare estimates across teams — did different groups arrive at similar numbers for the same stories? What drove the differences?

💡 Discussion Points

  • What happens when a team member consistently estimates much higher or lower than the group?
  • Should velocity be used as a performance metric? Why or why not?
  • How would you handle a story that the team cannot agree on (e.g., votes of 3, 5, and 21)?
  • What is the danger of committing to the "optimistic" forecast?
