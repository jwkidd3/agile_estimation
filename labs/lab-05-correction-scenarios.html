🔄 Lab 5: Correction Scenarios Workshop

Module 5 — Agile Project Estimation | Duration: 45 minutes (30 min work + 15 min presentations) | Style: Group Workshop

🎯 Lab Objectives

  • Apply correction techniques to realistic project scenarios
  • Use relative ROI to prioritize backlog items through group consensus
  • Practice stakeholder communication during course corrections

👥 Group Setup

Form groups of 4-5 people. Assign: Scrum Master (facilitates), Product Owner (advocates business impact), Tech Lead (technical perspective), Team Members (challenge ideas).

Tools: Whiteboard, spreadsheet, paper, or any combination.

Presentation: Each team presents scenario responses (3-5 min per team).

1 Scenario Response Drills

10 minutes

For each scenario, each role contributes their perspective. Document your team's action plan.

Scenario A: The Blow-Up Story

Mid-sprint, a 5-point story ("integrate with payment gateway") turns out to be far more complex than expected. The payment API docs are outdated — the team now estimates 21 points remain. Sprint capacity is 24 points total, and the team has already planned 22 points this sprint. This sprint is part of a release committed to a client for March 31. The project manager reported "on track" in last week's status meeting.

What actions do you take? What do you communicate, to whom, and how quickly? How do you update the release forecast, and what options do you present to protect the March 31 date?

Scenario B: The Scope Bomb

The CEO returns from a conference excited about AI chatbot capabilities and demands it be added. The team estimates 40 story points. The backlog is fully planned at current velocity. The CEO says: "I need the chatbot AND I need us to keep the Q2 release date."

How do you respond when both scope and date are fixed? What specific trade-off options do you present — with their date/scope/quality implications? Draft the actual response you'd give in a steering committee meeting.
