📐 Lab 1: Estimation Discipline Assessment

Module 1 — Agile Project Estimation | Duration: 60 minutes (45 min work + 15 min presentations) | Style: Group Workshop

🎯 Lab Objectives

  • Collaboratively assess estimation practices against Agile discipline standards
  • Identify gaps between current approaches and Agile estimation principles as a team
  • Experience the difference between people-driven and formula-driven estimation through group dynamics
  • Apply the "Agile Microscope" concept to a sample project scenario as a cross-functional group

👥 Group Setup

Form groups of 4-5 people. Agile estimation is a team discipline — it only works when practiced collaboratively. Every exercise in this lab is done as a group. Assign roles within your group:

  • Facilitator: Keeps the group on track and ensures everyone contributes
  • Scribe: Records the group's answers and key discussion points
  • Presenter: Shares the group's findings with the class
  • Timekeeper: Monitors time for each exercise

Tools & Presentation

Choose your tools: Your team decides how to collaborate — whiteboard, sticky notes, spreadsheet, digital tool, paper, or any combination. Use whatever works best for your group.

Class presentation: At the end of the lab, each team presents their key findings and insights to the class (3-5 minutes per team). Be prepared to explain your reasoning and what surprised you.

2 The Agile Microscope Exercise

15 minutes

Your group has been asked to estimate a project to build a new customer portal. Work together to apply the Agile Microscope at each level of planning — discuss as a team what level of detail is appropriate at each horizon.

Scenario

3 People vs Formulas Experiment

15 minutes

This exercise demonstrates why team estimation outperforms individual guesswork — a core Agile principle.

Round 1: Silent Individual Estimate (then group reveal)

Each group member independently writes down how many hours they think it would take to complete these tasks for the customer portal. Do not share your estimates yet — simultaneous reveal comes next.

  1. Build user login and authentication
  2. Create order history display page
  3. Implement support ticket submission form
  4. Build knowledge base search functionality
  5. Create admin dashboard for customer support team

Round 2: Simultaneous Reveal & Group Consensus

On the count of three, everyone reveals their estimates simultaneously. Discuss the differences — the person with the highest and lowest estimates must explain their reasoning. As a group, converge on a single consensus estimate for each task.

Round 3: Reflection

  • How wide was the range between individual estimates?
  • What assumptions were different between team members?
  • Did the group discussion surface risks or unknowns?
  • Is the group estimate more accurate than any individual estimate? Why?

4 Team Presentation to Class

15 minutes (3-5 min per team)

Each team presents their findings to the class. Cover:

  • Your group's top assessment gaps and why they matter
  • Your Agile Microscope planning levels for the customer portal
  • Key insights from the People vs Formulas experiment — what did your estimation ranges reveal?

After each presentation, other teams may ask questions or share different perspectives.

💡 Discussion Points

  • How does your assessment score map to estimation accuracy on your real projects?
  • What is the single biggest estimation discipline gap you identified?
  • How did the "people vs formulas" exercise demonstrate the wisdom of crowds?
  • At which planning level does your organization spend the most time? Is that appropriate?
